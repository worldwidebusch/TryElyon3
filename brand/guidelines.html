00 / THE MARKConstruction & rationale

The ascending E.

Elyon means the highest. The mark states this without saying it: a monogram E whose three arms lengthen as they rise. Read top-down it is a letter; read bottom-up it is an ascent: a level meter at maximum, an elevation reading on a drawing. It borrows from the vernacular the product already lives in (spec sheets, schematics, engineering plates) and from nothing else: no symbol, no metaphor, no ornament. One idea, held quietly.

The top arm, the highest line, is the only element that may carry Cobalt. Everything else is monochrome. This single accent is the entire expressive range of the mark.

01 / WORDMARKCustom letterforms · cap 60 · stroke 13

Five letters, one grid.

The wordmark is drawn, not typeset: five geometric monospaced letterforms on the same 13-unit stroke as the mark. The E carries the ascending arms (44/36/28), quieter than the monogram but unmistakably the same idea. The Y meets in a hard diagonal; the O is a rectangular ring; the N cuts one diagonal. Zero border radius throughout. It is a wordmark that could be milled from a plate.

IN RUNNING TEXT

The brand is written Elyon: capital E, lowercase after. Never ELYON in body copy, never "TryElyon" as a name. Try exists in exactly two places: the domain (tryelyon.com) and the trial call-to-action ("Try Elyon free"). Everywhere else, the company is simply Elyon.

DIGITAL FALLBACK

Where the drawn wordmark can't be used (plain text UI, documents), set ELYON in Martian Mono 800, uppercase, tracking −3%. This is a fallback, not a substitute; the drawn wordmark is canonical.

03 / COLORTwo surfaces, one ink, one accent

Paper, carbon, cobalt.

The palette is a drawing set: paper to draw on, carbon to print on, cobalt to annotate with. No gradients, ever. Signal red and verify green belong to the product's system states (errors, confirmations), never to marketing surfaces or the logo.

05 / USAGENon-negotiable

Always

  • Use the mark on paper or carbon, monochrome plus cobalt top arm.
  • Keep clearspace of at least one stroke width (13 units) around the mark.
  • Write the brand as "Elyon" in prose; "ELYON" only in the drawn wordmark and plates.
  • Reserve "Try" for the domain and the trial CTA exclusively.
  • Use hard edges, hairline-to-1.5px rules, zero border radius (except OS app-icon masks).
  • Let cobalt stay scarce: one accent per composition.

Never

  • No gradients, glows, shadows, bevels, or outlines on the mark.
  • No rotation, skew, or per-arm recoloring; only the top arm may be cobalt.
  • No robots, brains, circuits, sparkles, chat bubbles, or "AI" iconography.
  • No religious or symbolic references in explaining the name; the ascent is engineering, nothing more.
  • No placing the mark on photography or busy surfaces; paper or carbon only.
  • No "TryElyon" as a company name in any copy.

Voice. Elyon speaks like a well-written spec sheet: precise, calm, outcome-first. Quiet confidence means stating capability as fact and letting restraint do the persuading, the same discipline that governs the cobalt.
